Tips for financial Cybersecurity

How do account takeovers happen:

Account takeover starts with a scam email and asks for personal information such as account number , password and credit card informations. sometimes they call and pretend to be a representative of a financial instituition

signs there might be a problem in your account:

> missing statements

> missing funds

> unexpected notifications indicating a change to your account

Safeguarding tips:

> The best way to protect yourself from a malicious link is to make sure you don't click on any.

> Don’t share your passwords with others.Use a different password for each of your accounts, and change your passwords regularly.

> Enabling multifactor authentication (MFA) can significantly reduce the likelihood of a cybercriminal taking over your account.

> If possible, avoid using public computers or devices. Public computers might contain software that captures passwords and PINs, which others can then access

> Many public hotspots, such as wireless networks in airports, hotels and restaurants, reduce their security settings so it’s easier for individuals to access these networks

> Review your account activity and monthly account statements thoroughly as soon as they’re available. make sure your financial instituition has your current contact information
